The Sun

Type: G-type main-sequence star (G dwarf).

Diameter: Approximately 1.39 million km (864,000 miles), about 109 times that of Earth.

Mass: Roughly 1.99 × 10²⁴ kg (about 333,000 times the mass of Earth), making up about 99.86% of the total mass of the solar system.

Core Temperature: Around 15 million °C (27 million °F), where nuclear fusion occurs.

Surface Temperature: Approximately 5,500 °C (9,932 °F) on the photosphere (the visible surface).

Energy Production: The Sun generates energy through nuclear fusion, converting hydrogen into helium, which releases vast amounts of energy.

Solar Wind: A stream of charged particles released from the Sun's upper atmosphere, influencing space weather and Earth's magnetosphere.

Importance:

  • Provides light and heat essential for life on Earth.
  • Drives climate and weather patterns.
  • Influences the orbits of planets and other celestial bodies in the solar system.
